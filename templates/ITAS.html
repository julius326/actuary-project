Accumulation and discount factors

Accumulation factor is denoted by a(t) or A(t1, t2)

                a(t) = (Value @ t)/(Value @ 0) => (1*a(t))/1

                Simple : a(t) = (1+i*t)
                Compound : a(t) = (1+i)t

Discount factor is denoted by v(t)

                v(t) = (Value @ 0)/(Value @ t) = 1/a(t)

                Simple : a(t) = (1+it) =>  v(t) = 1/(1 + it)
                Compuond : a(t) = (1+i)t => v(t) = 1/(1+i)t = Vt
Example 
                    Sue takes a short term loan of 50,000/= .The lender charges a simple interest
                    of  8% p.a for 2 years .Find the
                    i) Accumulation factor
                    ii)The total amount to be repaid

                    sln
                    
                        i) a(t) = (1+i*t) = (1 + 0.08 * 2) = 1.16

                        ii) F = 50,000 * 1.16 = 58,0000/=
